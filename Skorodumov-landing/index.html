Physics

My inspiration for physics came from my father Boris G. Skorodumov who was a nuclear physicist. He published multiple articles in peer reviewed journals. The one which impacted me the most is "Determination of Hydrogen in Materials, Nuclear Physics Methods, 1989”.

I was invited to a summer school at Novosibirsk State University where I took math classes at Summer School of NSU. It gave me a solid foundation in mathematics and allow me to continue my journey at Lyceum # 1511 where I specialized in physics and mathematics.

As an undergraduate student at Moscow Engineering Physics Institute, I participated in multiple nuclear physics research projects at Kurchatov Institute, Joint Institute for Nuclear Research and University of Jyvaskyla. I was invited by DESY and GSI organizations to international summer programs where I had an exposure to particle physics. I am very grateful to Prof. Vladilen Goldberg who inspired me and was my mentor at this period of my life.

DESY International Student Program, Berlin, Germany, 2002
GSI International Student Program, Darmstadt, Germany, 2000

As a graduate student at University of Notre Dame, I specialized in low energy experimental nuclear physics. My research focused on structure of light nuclei, structure of exotic neutron-rich and neutron-deficient isotopes, clustering phenomena in nuclei, mechanisms of nuclear reactions, applications of nuclear physics methods for determining rates of nuclear reactions that drive nucleosynthesis. I am very grateful to Prof. Grigory Rogachev who provided guidance and support during my Ph.D. studies.

Ph.D Thesis, “Resonance Reactions Induced by Light Radioactive Beams”, University of Notre Dame, 2007

At this stage, my formal association with nuclear physics has ended and I started the preparation for the world of quantitative finance.
